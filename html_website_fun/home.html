About

I'm a PhD student at the University of Oxford in the Centre for Applied Superconductivity, having received an MSc in Nuclear Science and Engineering from the University of Bristol, and a BEng in Materials Engineering from Exeter University.

My research investigates the effect of radiation damage on high temperature superconductors. I conduct irradiation experiments, characterise the effects on superconducting performance and changes to the atomic structure of the material, and validate with computational models using the density-functional theory code CASTEP.

The main applications of my research are:

  • Magnets for nuclear fusion energy
  • Magnets for particle accelerators (such as the high luminosity upgrade at CERN)
  • Space and marine propulsion, resistanceless energy transmission, and many types of superconducting machines

On this website I will share details of my academic research as well as some other writings.
